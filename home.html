About Lewis

LH44

A Racing Legend

Sir Lewis Carl Davidson Hamilton born January 7, 1985, in Stevenage, England, is widely regarded as the greatest Formula 1 driver in history. Starting his F1 journey with McLaren in 2007, Hamilton immediately made waves by becoming the youngest World Champion at the time.

Throughout his illustrious career, he has dominated the sport with unprecedented achievements, breaking countless records and redefining what's possible in motorsport. His relentless pursuit of excellence extends beyond the track, as he continues to be a powerful voice for equality and environmental sustainability.

Beyond the Track

A champion who uses his platform for change

🌍

Environmental Advocacy

Hamilton has been vocal about climate change and sustainability. He has advocated for sustainable practices in Formula 1 and launched Team X44 in the Extreme E championship, promoting electric racing and environmental awareness.

✊

Racial Equality

As one of the few Black athletes in motorsport, Hamilton has been a powerful voice for diversity and inclusion. He established the Hamilton Commission to increase representation in motorsport and continues to fight against racial injustice.

💪

Philanthropy

Through the Hamilton Foundation and various initiatives, he has supported education, healthcare, and disaster relief efforts. His charitable work spans globally, making a positive impact on countless lives.

🏆

Sportsmanship

Known for his graciousness in victory and defeat, Hamilton has mentored young drivers and shown exemplary sportsmanship throughout his career, setting a standard for future generations.
